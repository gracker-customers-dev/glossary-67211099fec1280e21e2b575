Wallet

A digital or physical tool used to store, manage, and facilitate transactions of non-human identities.

Description

In the context of non-human identities, a 'wallet' refers to a system or service that enables the storage and management of various forms of digital assets or credentials associated with non-human entities such as bots, applications, or devices. These wallets can hold cryptocurrencies, tokens, digital certificates, or any other form of identity verification required for transactions. Non-human wallets are crucial in ecosystems where automated agents interact with blockchain technologies, IoT devices, or any digital platforms requiring identity validation. This allows non-human entities to autonomously engage in transactions, access services, and maintain security without human intervention. With the rise of decentralized applications (dApps) and automated systems, the functionality of non-human wallets is evolving, providing increased security, efficiency, and ease of use in digital economic interactions. They can also be designed to comply with various regulatory frameworks, ensuring that non-human transactions are secure and identified.

Examples

  • A cryptocurrency wallet used by an automated trading bot to execute trades.
  • An IoT device wallet that stores credentials for accessing cloud services.

Additional Information

  • Non-human wallets can be integrated with smart contracts for automated transactions.
  • Security features may include multi-signature verification or biometric authentication.
