Decentralized Identifier (DID)

A new type of identifier that enables verifiable, self-sovereign digital identities.

Description

Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs) are a new form of digital identifier that allows the creation of verifiable and self-sovereign identities on the internet. Unlike traditional identifiers, which are often managed by centralized authorities (such as email providers or social networks), DIDs are designed to be fully decentralized and can be created and controlled by the subject of the identifier themselves. DIDs are part of a broader movement towards self-sovereign identity, where individuals and entities have complete control over their own identities without reliance on intermediaries. They are typically represented in a machine-readable format and can be associated with public keys, enabling secure authentication and interaction in digital contexts. DIDs are defined by W3C (World Wide Web Consortium) standards and can be used across various domains, including IoT devices, online services, and more. The use of DIDs is gaining traction in various industries ranging from finance to healthcare, providing a means to establish trust and privacy in digital interactions.

Examples

  • A DID could represent a digital wallet for a cryptocurrency user, allowing them to control their funds without relying on a bank.
  • A DID could be used by an IoT device to authenticate itself to other devices and services on the network.

Additional Information

  • DIDs can be resolved into decentralized data registries, which store associated public keys and other metadata.
  • DIDs can help combat identity theft and fraud by allowing individuals to manage their own identities.
